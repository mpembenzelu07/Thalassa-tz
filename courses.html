FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Find answers to common questions about our diving courses

What are the prerequisites for taking a diving course?

+

For beginner courses like Discover Scuba and Open Water Diver, you must be at least 10 years old, in good physical health, and able to swim. You'll need to complete a medical questionnaire. For advanced courses, you must hold the prerequisite certification (e.g., Open Water certification for Advanced Open Water).

Is diving safe for beginners?

+

Yes, scuba diving is very safe when taught by certified professionals. Our instructors follow PADI standards and maintain a maximum 4:1 student-to-instructor ratio for safety. All equipment is regularly serviced and maintained to the highest standards.

What equipment do I need to bring?

+

We provide all necessary diving equipment including wetsuit, BCD, regulator, tanks, weights, and mask. You only need to bring your swimwear, towel, and sunscreen. If you prefer to use your own mask, snorkel, or fins, you're welcome to bring them.

How long does PADI certification last?

+

PADI certifications are lifetime certifications that never expire. However, if you haven't dived in a while (more than 6-12 months), we recommend taking a Scuba Review or ReActivate program to refresh your skills before diving again.

Can I get a refund if I need to cancel?

+

We offer full refunds for cancellations made at least 7 days before the course start date. For cancellations within 7 days, we offer course credit that can be used within one year. No-shows are non-refundable. We recommend travel insurance for unexpected circumstances.
